Saturday, April 12, 2025

Fun Lesson on Finance

Kids learned about financial management through interactive play shopping activities.

We taught the kids about understanding important financial needs, like primary needs, secondary needs, clothing, and food needs. After the explanation, we jumped straight into practice. We had prepared engaging activities such as play shopping, where the kids could have a shopping simulation for their needs. The experiences and interactions from this session will leave a lasting impression and help the children develop a better understanding of financial decision-making. We are very proud of them for coming this far!

Saturday, March 22, 2025

Counting Money

Interactive session teaching children how to count and manage money.

This time, we gave them story exercises in the form of questions about shopping. Based on the story and context, they had to answer the questions. These types of questions were harder to do because it doesn't only require counting skills but also reading and understanding.

After the kids finished, we moved on to the next activity. We divided the kids into 2 groups. In this activity, we gave the kids some questions, and the first group to answer would get a point. It was quite competitive. By the end of it, the group with the most points would win.

Saturday, March 15, 2025

Learning Math the Fun Way

Creative math games that made learning arithmetic enjoyable for children.

On this visit, our main material was about math. We divided the class into 2 groups. 1 group consisted of kindergarten kids and the other elementary kids. We introduced the little kids to numbers from 1 to 10 and also taught them about simple addition. Meanwhile, for the elementary kids, we taught them how to do multiplication of 2-digit and 3-digit numbers.

After teaching and reviewing the material, we gave them all a piece of paper and wrote down on the whiteboard 10 multiplication questions. Then we told them to write their answers on the piece of paper. To be honest, we were quite shocked and also glad to see how the kids were able to do them pretty quickly and well.

Saturday, February 22, 2025

Importance of Teamwork

Group activities demonstrating the value of collaboration and teamwork.

We started the session with a newspaper game, where each group got the chance to answer questions given to them as fast as they could. The challenge is, each group had to answer a question given by us correctly in order for the newspaper to remain big enough for everyone to stand on comfortably. Otherwise, the newspaper would be folded in half to which the kids would have to squish around and stay within the newspaper.

Aside from the newspaper game, there was another game where all the kids had to hold hands while switching places without letting go of each other's hands. Once the "human knot" has been made, the kids had to find a way to get back to their original position, while still holding hands.
